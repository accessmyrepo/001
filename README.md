# Datasets Used

This project utilizes the following publicly available image forgery detection datasets.
This repository includes processed versions of publicly available datasets (CASIA, CoMoFoD, CG-1050) used strictly for academic and research purposes.
All original datasets are the property of their respective authors and institutions.

The datasets were obtained from their official sources and have been reorganized and preprocessed for the purpose of our research.
This repository does not claim ownership of the original data and it's only for academic use.

## 1. CASIA v1.0 and CASIA v2.0
Source: Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences
https://github.com/namtpham/casia2groundtruth

### Citation:
J. Dong, W. Wang, and T. Tan, “CASIA Image Tampering Detection Evaluation Database,” in Proceedings of the 2013 IEEE China Summit and International Conference on Signal and Information Processing (ChinaSIP), 2013, pp. 422–426.

## 2. CG-1050 Dataset
Source: Data in Brief (Elsevier)
Paper: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.dib.2019.103819
https://data.mendeley.com/datasets/dk84bmnyw9/2

### Citation:
Castro, Maikol; Ballesteros L, Dora Maria; Renza, Diego (2019), “CG-1050: Original and tampered images (Color and grayscale)”, Mendeley Data, V2, doi: 10.17632/dk84bmnyw9.2

## 3. CoMoFoD (Copy-Move Forgery Detection Dataset)
Source: University of Zagreb
Download: http://www.vcl.fer.hr/comofod/comofod.html

### Citation:
D. Tralic, I. Zupancic, S. Grgic, and M. Grgic, “CoMoFoD – New Database for Copy-Move Forgery Detection,” in Proceedings of the 55th International Symposium ELMAR, 2013, pp. 49–54.
